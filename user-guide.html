The Complete Guide

Master your risk, manage your psychology, and understand the mathematics behind RiskDesk.

Core Philosophy

RiskDesk is not just a calculator; it is a discipline engine. It is built on the premise that Capital Preservation is the primary job of a trader.

RiskDesk delivers dynamic, risk-first, multi-account position sizing with fx conversion support paired with seamless trade logging, sharing, and outcome recording. It combines comprehensive trading insight and statistical analysis with a powerful journaling engine—featuring trade rule capture and pre-populated activity streams—to help you trade with precision and discipline.

1. Initial Setup

Account Configuration

Go to Settings to define your trading environment. You can add multiple accounts (e.g., "Main Swing", "Day Trading").

  • Master Account: You must select one account as "Master" using the radio button. This account's size is used as the denominator for "Equity Contribution (EC%)" calculations, allowing you to track how individual trades impact your total net worth.

Currency & FX API

Set your Global Base Currency (e.g., GBP). If you trade US stocks using a non-USD account, enable the FX API. RiskDesk will automatically fetch real-time rates (e.g., GBP/USD) to ensure your position sizing is accurate based on your base currency risk. You can also manually override this rate.

Strategy Manager

Create custom tags for your setups (e.g., "Gap & Go", "Reversal", "Breakout"). These tags are used throughout the app to filter Logs and analyze performance in the Stats section.

Stats Configuration: Partials Impact

Found in Settings, this toggle controls how your statistics are calculated. It determines whether you view your data based on "Banked Cash" or "Current Performance".

Feature OFF (Strict Mode) ON (Dynamic Mode)
Math Calculates averages using ONLY fully closed trades. Includes Realized P&L from active trades (Partials).
Use Case Conservative. "What is my proven, banked edge?" Cashflow. "How is my account growing right now?"

Data Management

  • Backup & Restore: RiskDesk is Offline-First. Download your entire history as a JSON file regularly using the Backup button.
  • Danger Zone: Located at the bottom of Settings. This performs a Factory Reset, deleting all Logs, Accounts, and Journal entries. This cannot be undone.

2. The Calculator Engine

The calculator determines your position size based on your risk tolerance. It offers two distinct mathematical models:

Model A: Equity Risk %

"I want to lose exactly 1.0% of my account if I'm wrong."

Shares = (Account * Risk%) / (Entry - Stop)

Model B: Position Size %

"I want to put 10% of my capital into this stock."

Shares = (Account * Alloc%) / Entry

Key Features

  • Trade Currency: Ensure you select the currency of the asset you are trading. If it differs from your Base Currency, the FX API will handle the conversion for accurate sizing.
  • Staggered Stops: You are not limited to a single stop price. Toggle "Staggered" to set multiple weighted stop levels. The system calculates a weighted average stop to determine true risk per share.
  • Context & Strategy: Select a Strategy tag and add context notes (e.g., "Market choppy") before logging. This is crucial for the Insights engine.
  • Chart Capture: Paste a chart image directly from your clipboard (Ctrl+V) into the "Paste Zone" to save the setup chart permanently with the trade.

4. Trade Management

Tap any log entry to open the Management Console. This is where you manage the lifecycle of the trade.

  • Stop Adjustments: Update your "Current Stop" as the trade moves in your favor. This automatically updates your Open Heat metric on the dashboard.
  • Partial Exits: Record partial sells by specifying Price, Quantity, and Date. The system calculates the realized P&L for that specific chunk and reduces your NER (Net Exposure Risk). You can also Edit or Undo exits here.
  • Chart Gallery: Stores all images associated with the trade (Entry Chart, Exit Chart, and any Journal updates). Tap any image to view it full screen.

Dynamic De-Risking

The "Risk Free" Formula

RiskDesk uses a dynamic equation to tell you exactly where to take partial profits to eliminate risk. Unlike static rules (e.g., "Sell 1/3 at 2R"), RiskDesk adapts to your Adjusted Stop.

Target = Entry + ( (CurrentNER / SharesToSell) - CurrentRiskPerShare )

Consistency Heatmap

This grid visualizes your "Origination Quality." You can configure how you want the Consistency grid to plot your trades.

Plot trades based on their Entry Date. If a trade entered on Monday turns green, Monday lights up green. It answers: "How good were my entry decisions on that day?"

Plot trades based on their Exit Date. If a partial on a profitable partial or overall trade is closed Monday then Monday turns green. It can help show: "How effective are my exits? Am I securing profits to help finance new risk? Should I be sizing up or down?

Plot trades based on your Discipline. Have you adhered to Your Rules?. If Yes the day is marked Green, if No the day is marked Red. Use in conjunction with the other Consistency Heatmap settings to see "How discipline impacts my trading outcomes?"

Note: The heatmap uses UTC dates to ensure consistency across timezones. Trades made late at night may appear on the following day.

Key Formulas

R-Multiple

Standardizes profit/loss relative to initial risk.

R = (ExitPrice - EntryPrice) / (EntryPrice - StopPrice)
Expectancy

The average R-value you can expect per trade over time.

Expectancy = (Win% × AvgWinR) - (Loss% × AvgLossR)
Profit Factor

A measure of system sustainability.

PF = (Gross Profit $) / (Gross Loss $)

PF > 2.0 is excellent. PF < 1.0 means you are losing money.

6. The Journal

The Journal tab is designed for daily review and behavioral tracking.

Features

  • Rules & Philosophy: Define your "Golden Rules" in the settings tab. These persist and serve as your daily checklist.
  • Automated Daily Log: The Journal automatically pulls in every trade, partial exit, and P&L event for the selected date.
  • Discipline Tracking: You must answer: "Did you follow your rules today?"
    • YES: Marks the day Green on the consistency heatmap.
    • NO: Marks the day Red. A checklist of your defined rules appears, allowing you to specify exactly which rules you broke. This tracks behavioral leaks over time.
  • Review: Tag your Emotions (Anxious, Bored, Focused), write notes, and paste chart images.

Glossary

R-Multiple
A unit of risk. 1R represents your intended risk amount on a trade. If you risk $100 and make $300, that is a +3R trade.
Scratch
A trade that results in a near-breakeven outcome (between -0.1R and +0.1R). RiskDesk filters these out of "Win/Loss" magnitude averages to prevent data noise.
NER
Net Exposure Risk. The amount of open risk that is NOT yet financed by realized profits. It is the true measure of your portfolio's vulnerability.
Heat
Open Heat. The gross sum of risk across all open positions. If you have 4 trades open, each risking 1% of equity, your Heat is 4%.
