The Complete Guide

Master your risk, manage your psychology, and understand the mathematics behind RiskDesk.

Core Philosophy

RiskDesk is not just a calculator; it is a discipline engine. It is built on the premise that Capital Preservation is the primary job of a trader.

Most journals track results (Lagging Indicators). RiskDesk tracks Exposure (Leading Indicators). We focus on "Open Heat" and "NER" (New Exposure Risk) to ensure you never blow up your account, regardless of market conditions.

1. Initial Setup

Account Configuration

Go to Settings to define your trading environment. You can add multiple accounts (e.g., "Main Swing", "Day Trading", "Challenge Account").

  • Base Currency: The currency you want to see your total wealth in (e.g., GBP).
  • Trade Currency: The currency of the assets you trade (e.g., USD for US Stocks). RiskDesk handles the FX conversion automatically.
  • Master Account: Select one account as "Master". All new trade logs will be attributed to this account for P&L tracking.

Defining Strategies

Don't just log "Long AAPL". Log why. Create strategy tags like Gap & Go, 21d Pullback, or Earnings Flag in Settings. This allows the AI Analyst to later tell you which strategies are profitable and which are burning cash.

2. The Calculator Engine

The calculator determines your position size based on your risk tolerance. It offers two distinct mathematical models:

Model A: Equity Risk %

"I want to lose exactly 1.0% of my account if I'm wrong."

Shares = (Account * Risk%) / (Entry - Stop)

Best for: Daily risk management and consistency.

Model B: Position Size %

"I want to put 10% of my capital into this stock."

Shares = (Account * Alloc%) / Entry

Best for: Long term investing or fixed allocation models.

Staggered Stops

You are not limited to a single stop price. Toggle "Staggered" to set up to 4 weighted stop levels (e.g., "Sell 50% at $100, 50% at $98"). The system calculates a Weighted Average Stop Price to determine your true risk per share.

3. Execution & Data Entry

Bulk Import (CSV)

Migrating from another journal? Use the Import feature in Settings. Download the template provided, paste your broker data (Date, Ticker, Entry), and manually add your Stop Loss and Strategy. RiskDesk requires the Stop Loss to calculate R-Multiples.

Image Pasting

Visual memory is powerful. You can paste screenshots directly from your clipboard (Ctrl+V) into the "Paste Zone" on the calculator or management screen. Images are stored locally in your browser database.

4. Trade Management

This is where RiskDesk outshines standard spreadsheets. It manages the lifecycle of the trade.

Dynamic De-Risking

The "Risk Free" Formula

RiskDesk uses a dynamic equation to tell you exactly where to take partial profits to eliminate risk. Unlike static rules (e.g., "Sell 1/3 at 2R"), RiskDesk adapts to your Adjusted Stop.

Target = Entry + ( (CurrentNER / SharesToSell) - CurrentRiskPerShare )

If you tighten your stop, your risk decreases, so your take-profit target moves closer to price. The system rewards you for good defense.

The Dashboard Metrics

  • HEAT
    Open Heat (Gross Exposure)
    The "Worst Case Scenario." If every open trade hit its stop simultaneously, this is the % of equity you would lose. Keep this below 6%.
  • NER
    New Exposure Risk (Unfinanced Risk)
    NER = (RemainingShares × RiskDistance) - BankedProfit
    This metric tracks risk that hasn't been paid for yet. Once you bank enough profit to cover the potential loss on remaining shares, NER drops to 0.00%.

5. Analytics & Mathematics

Strict vs. Dynamic Mode

In Settings, you can toggle "Include Partials in Averages." This fundamentally changes how your performance is viewed:

Metric Strict Mode (Default) Dynamic Mode
Win Rate Only fully closed trades count. Includes active trades with partials. (Volatile)
Avg Win Clean average of completed outcomes. Lower. Dragged down by small partials.
Net R Always sums all realized cash. Always sums all realized cash.

Consistency Heatmap

This grid visualizes your "Origination Quality." It plots trades based on their Entry Date. If a trade entered on Monday turns green, Monday lights up green. It answers: "How good were my decisions on that day?"

Note: The heatmap uses UTC dates to ensure consistency across timezones. Trades made late at night may appear on the following day.

Key Formulas

R-Multiple

Standardizes profit/loss relative to initial risk.

R = (ExitPrice - EntryPrice) / (EntryPrice - StopPrice)
Expectancy

The average R-value you can expect per trade over time.

Expectancy = (Win% × AvgWinR) - (Loss% × AvgLossR)
Profit Factor

A measure of system sustainability.

PF = (Gross Profit $) / (Gross Loss $)

PF > 2.0 is excellent. PF < 1.0 means you are losing money.

Glossary

R-Multiple
A unit of risk. 1R represents your intended risk amount on a trade. If you risk $100 and make $300, that is a +3R trade.
Scratch
A trade that results in a near-breakeven outcome (between -0.1R and +0.1R). RiskDesk filters these out of "Win/Loss" magnitude averages to prevent data noise.
NER
Net Exposure Risk. The amount of open risk that is NOT yet financed by realized profits. It is the true measure of your portfolio's vulnerability.
Heat
Open Heat. The gross sum of risk across all open positions. If you have 4 trades open, each risking 1% of equity, your Heat is 4%.
